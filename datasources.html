Data Sources for Synthesis


General Data Sources

  • Environmental Data Initiative EDI: Very wide variety of data (mostly research).
  • Data Portal: A list of open data portals. This is a very wide variety of datasets (includes socioeconomic and other datasets in addition to environmental).
  • National Forest Service: Datasets for silviculture and biodiversity.
  • US EPA: air quality, environmental chemistry (Soil, Groundwater, and Freshwaters).
  • EPA Enterprise Data Catalog: Another set of data maintained by EPA.
  • data.world: General repository of data.
  • Resource Watch
  • NOAA One Stop: Data from National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.
  • UNEP Pollution Data: United National Environmental Program (UNEP) pollution data.

Specific Projects

  • NEON (National Environmental Observation Network): Wide variety of data from more than 3 dozen sites across North America.

Animal Movement

  • Movebank: Dataset of animal tracking data using GPS/location trackers.

Camera Traps

  • Labeled Information Library of Alexandria: Biology and Conservation: Trail camera images for a variety of wildlife and habitats worldwide.
  • CaltechCameraTraps: Hundreds of thousands of images from a variety of projects.
  • Wildlife Insights
  • Florida Wildlife Camera Trap Dataset: https://www.crcv.ucf.edu/research/projects/florida-wildlife-camera-trap-dataset/.

Biodiversity

  • Nature Serve

Vegetation

  • Veg Bank

Geospatial (GIS) Data

  • Google Earth Engine: Wide variety of satellite geospatial data.
  • Soil Grids: Global dataset of soil properties.
  • ESRI Data

Air Quality/Climate

  • Purple Air Network: Air quality sensor network run by communities.
  • US EPA Air Quality: Air quality data synthesis (includes Purple Air data for some datasets).
  • Climate Trace: Greenhouse gas data (global).
  • Data Hub: Archive of climate data.
  • NCAR Data: Atmospheric data from the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR).

Water Quality & Hydrology

  • StreamPulse: Water chemistry sensor data from a national and global network.
  • US Geological Survey National Water Information System: National dataset of hydrology and water quality for lakes, rivers, streams, and groundwater.

Policy

  • International Environmental Agreements Database Project: Includes a lot of policy/government information.
  • Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) Environmental Data
